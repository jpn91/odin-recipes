Ribeye

Description

A reverse seared Ribeye, basted with butter, garlic, and thyme. Truly a meal fit for a king.

Ingredients

  • Ribeye Steak
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic Powder
  • Butter
  • Thyme
  • Garlic Clove

Steps

  1. Take the ribeye steak and cover it in coarse salt. Make sure you get all the edges. Place the steak on a cooling rack on a tray and put it in the fridge. Leave the steak in the fridge for at least 3 hours. The longer you leave the steak in the fridge, the beefier it will taste. This is a process called dry-brining
  2. Preheat the oven for 225 Degrees farenheit.
  3. Season the steak with the garlic powder and pepper.
  4. Place the steak in the oven. Depending on desired doneness, cook the steak in the oven anywhere from 15 to 30 minutes.
  5. When the steak is almost done cooking in the oven, begin heating a cast-iron pan on the stove.
  6. When the steak is done cooking in the oven, place the steak in the cast iron pan
  7. Add the butter, garlic, and thyme to the pan with the steak
  8. Baste the steak and flip it as often as needed to make sure it is cooked evenly and has developed a good crust
  9. After about 2 minutes of flipping and basting, remove the steak to a cutting board and allow it to rest for 10 minutes
  10. Open a bottle of red wine and pour a glass! Time to eat!
